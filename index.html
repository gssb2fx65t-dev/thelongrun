Sleep
Sleep is the highest-leverage thing
Your body does not get fitter during runs - it adapts during recovery, and that process is almost entirely sleep-dependent. During heavy training weeks (20+ miles) you likely need 30-60 min more than usual.
Two nights before matters most
The night before Saturday long run matters less than Wednesday and Thursday nights. One bad Friday does not derail a long run nearly as much as poor mid-week sleep.
Alcohol and sleep quality
Alcohol disrupts sleep depth even when it does not reduce hours. You get the hours but not the restorative depth. Particularly relevant the night before any hard effort.
Nutrition
Protein timing
The 30-60 min window after Saturday long run is when muscles are most receptive to protein. Aim for 25-40g in that window. During heavier training weeks, target 100-130g protein per day total.
Do not run fasted on long runs
As Saturday distances grow past 7-8 miles, running without breakfast compromises quality and recovery. A simple breakfast 60-90 minutes before is enough. Do not skip this.
Carbs are your fuel
During weeks above 15 miles, carbohydrates are your primary fuel. Restricting them makes runs harder, slows recovery, and raises injury risk. The grain pouches in meal prep are doing important work.
Eat enough
Appetite suppresses after long runs so under-fueling is common. On days with a long run you need meaningfully more food than rest days.
Hydration & Fueling
When to start
Hydration awareness starts now. Mid-run fueling threshold: Week 14 (Saturday hits 8 miles) — start experimenting. Essential by Week 17 (Saturday at 9 miles, over 90 minutes). Heat block Week 21: shift to active pre-hydration starting Thursday evening.
Tailwind as an all-in-one
Handles hydration and fueling simultaneously. Mix 2 scoops in 20oz water, drink every 15-20 minutes from mile 1. Gentler on the stomach than most gels. One bottle covers a 90-minute effort.
The 36-hour protocol
Thursday evening: familiar dinner, lean protein, white rice or pasta, low fat, low fiber. Friday: carbohydrate-forward meals, light electrolyte drink afternoon, dinner by 7pm. Saturday breakfast 60-75 min before: plain bagel with peanut butter and banana, or oatmeal. 16-20oz water with breakfast. Nothing new in the entire 36-hour window.
Bottles that do not swish
Nathan SpeedDraw Plus 18oz — strap holds bottle against palm, bite valve prevents sloshing. ~$30. Soft flasks collapse as you drink, eliminating sloshing entirely. Nathan Pinnacle belt or Ultimate Direction FK for high-heat long runs when you want hands free.
